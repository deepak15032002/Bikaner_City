Bhandasar Jain Temple, also known as Bhandashah Jain Temple, is one of the most exquisite and revered Jain temples located in Bikaner, Rajasthan. Dedicated to Sumatinatha, the fifth Tirthankara of Jainism, this temple is renowned for its stunning architecture, intricate carvings, and rich history.
Historical Background-
The construction of Bhandasar Jain Temple began in 1468 and was completed in 1514. It was commissioned by a wealthy Jain merchant named Bhandasa Oswal, after whom the temple is named. According to local legend, around 40,000 kilograms of ghee (clarified butter) were used instead of water in the mortar mix during the temple’s construction, which is believed to contribute to its durability and unique charm.
Architectural Splendor-
Bhandasar Jain Temple is a fine example of traditional Jain architecture, combined with the artistic styles prevalent in the region during its construction. The temple is adorned with detailed frescoes, vibrant paintings, and intricate stone carvings, each contributing to its overall splendor.
Key Features:
Frescoes and Paintings: The walls and ceilings of the temple are adorned with beautiful frescoes depicting scenes from Jain mythology and everyday life. These vibrant paintings are detailed and well-preserved, showcasing the artistic excellence of the period.
Intricate Carvings: The temple features exquisite stone carvings, especially on the pillars and ceilings. The carvings include floral patterns, geometric designs, and depictions of Jain deities and motifs, all carved with remarkable precision.
Three-Storied Structure: The temple is built in three levels, with each floor showcasing different aspects of Jain art and architecture. The top floor offers a panoramic view of Bikaner city, adding to the temple's charm.
Marble Pillars: The temple is supported by intricately carved marble pillars that add to its grandeur. These pillars are not only structurally significant but also artistically decorated, enhancing the temple's aesthetic appeal.
Sanctum Sanctorum: The innermost sanctum houses the idol of Lord Sumatinatha, made of white marble. The serene and divine ambiance of the sanctum attracts devotees and visitors alike.
Religious Significance-
Bhandasar Jain Temple holds immense religious importance for the Jain community. It is an active place of worship and pilgrimage, drawing devotees from across the country. The temple also plays a significant role during Jain festivals and rituals, making it a vibrant center of religious activity.
Visiting Bhandasar Jain Temple-
Visitors to Bikaner are often drawn to Bhandasar Jain Temple for its architectural beauty and spiritual ambiance. The temple is open to tourists throughout the year, and it is advisable to dress modestly and respect the sanctity of the place. Photography is often restricted inside the temple, so it’s best to check the guidelines before visiting.
