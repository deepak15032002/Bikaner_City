The Karni Mata Temple, also known as the Temple of Rats, is one of the most unique and intriguing religious sites in India. Located in the town of Deshnoke, about 30 kilometers from Bikaner, Rajasthan, this temple is dedicated to Karni Mata, a revered female sage and deity believed to be an incarnation of the goddess Durga.
Historical Background-
Karni Mata, also known as Karniji, lived during the 14th century and was a mystic who was considered a reincarnation of the goddess Durga by her followers. She was known for her miraculous deeds and is deeply revered by the local community. The temple was originally constructed in the early 20th century by Maharaja Ganga Singh of Bikaner, although the legend of Karni Mata dates back much further.
Unique Feature: The Rats-
What makes the Karni Mata Temple truly unique is its population of rats. The temple is home to thousands of rats, which are considered sacred and are called "kabbas." These rats freely roam the temple premises and are revered by devotees.
Key Beliefs and Legends:
- Reincarnation Belief: According to legend, Karni Mata’s stepson, Laxman, drowned in a pond while attempting to drink water. Karni Mata implored Yama, the god of death, to revive him. Yama initially refused but eventually relented, allowing Laxman and all of Karni Mata’s male children to be reincarnated as rats.
- Sacred Rats: The rats are believed to be the reincarnated souls of Karni Mata’s devotees. Killing a rat, even accidentally, is considered highly inauspicious. If a rat is killed, it must be replaced with a rat made of silver or gold.
Architectural Features-
The Karni Mata Temple showcases impressive Mughal-style architecture with beautiful marble and silver work. The temple's entrance is adorned with intricate marble carvings and solid silver doors. Inside, the temple has a silver gate and a beautiful idol of Karni Mata.
Key Attractions:
- Holy Rats: The primary attraction of the temple is the large population of rats. Devotees consider it auspicious if a rat runs over their feet. Spotting a white rat among the multitude of black rats is considered highly fortunate as they are believed to be manifestations of Karni Mata and her sons.
- Festivals and Rituals: The temple is especially vibrant during the Navratri festival and Karni Mata fair, attracting thousands of devotees. Special prayers and rituals are conducted, and the temple is beautifully decorated.
- Sanctum Sanctorum: The main shrine houses the idol of Karni Mata adorned with a garland and a crown. Devotees offer food to the rats, believing that the offerings will be blessed by the goddess.
Visiting Karni Mata Temple-
The Karni Mata Temple is open to visitors throughout the year. When visiting, it is important to respect the sanctity of the temple and the beliefs of the devotees. Wearing modest clothing and removing shoes before entering the temple premises is customary. Visitors should also be prepared to encounter a large number of rats and to tread carefully to avoid stepping on them.
Conclusion-
The Karni Mata Temple is not only a place of religious significance but also a cultural marvel that offers a unique glimpse into the local customs and beliefs of Rajasthan. Its unusual inhabitants, the sacred rats, make it one of the most extraordinary temples in India, drawing tourists, pilgrims, and curious travelers from around the world. Whether you are there to seek blessings, witness the unique traditions, or simply explore an intriguing aspect of Indian culture, the Karni Mata Temple provides an unforgettable experience.
