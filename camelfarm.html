The National Research Centre on Camel (NRCC) is a premier institute located in Bikaner, Rajasthan, India, dedicated to the research and development of camels. Here are some key details about NRCC:

Overview-
- Establishment: The NRCC was established in 1984 under the Indian Council of Agricultural Research (ICAR) to promote and carry out research on various aspects of camel biology, health, and management.
- Location: It is situated about 8 kilometers from the city center of Bikaner, making it accessible for visitors and researchers.

Objectives-
- Research: The primary objective of NRCC is to conduct research on camel breeding, genetics, health, nutrition, and management practices to improve camel productivity and health.
- Extension Services: The center provides training and extension services to camel breeders, farmers, and other stakeholders to disseminate knowledge and best practices.
- Conservation: It focuses on the conservation of camel breeds, especially those that are indigenous to India, to ensure their sustainability and protection.

Facilities and Research Areas-
- Laboratories: Equipped with modern laboratories for conducting research in animal genetics, reproduction, nutrition, and disease management.
- Camel Herd: Maintains a large herd of different camel breeds for research purposes, including Bikaneri, Jaisalmeri, Kachchi, and Mewari breeds.
- Veterinary Hospital: Provides healthcare services to camels and serves as a training center for veterinary professionals.
- Library: A well-stocked library with a wide range of literature on camel research and related subjects.

Visitor Information-
- Tourist Attraction: NRCC is a popular tourist attraction in Bikaner. Visitors can tour the facilities, learn about camels, and observe various aspects of camel management.
- Camel Milk Products: The center also showcases and sells various camel milk products, such as camel milk, ice cream, and cosmetics made from camel milk.
- Guided Tours: Guided tours are available for visitors, providing detailed insights into the research activities and the significance of camels in the region.

Educational and Cultural Significance-
- Educational Programs: NRCC conducts various educational programs and workshops for students, researchers, and farmers to educate them about the importance of camels in agriculture and their role in the desert ecosystem.
- Cultural Role: Camels are an integral part of the culture and economy of Rajasthan, and the NRCC plays a crucial role in preserving and promoting this heritage.

NRCC in Bikaner is not only a center for advanced research but also an educational and cultural hub that highlights the importance of camels in Rajasthan and beyond.

Visiting Hours
Timings: The NRCC is generally open to visitors from 2:00 PM to 6:00 PM in the summer and from 12:00 PM to 6:00 PM in the winter. Closed: The center may be closed on certain holidays, so it's advisable to check ahead of your visit.

Activities and Attractions
Camel Rides: Visitors can enjoy camel rides around the center.
Camel Museum: The museum at the center provides detailed information about camels, their biology, and their role in the desert ecosystem.
Camel Milk Products: Visitors can try camel milk and products made from camel milk, such as ice cream and sweets.
Souvenir Shop: A shop at the center sells camel-related products and souvenirs.
